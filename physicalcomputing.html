Charlotte the Spiderbot

Write up: Evolution and Construction of the Spiderbot - 2017

The Spiderbot is a toy prototype created for the Fall 2017 course Info 4320 - Rapid Prototyping and Physical Computing at Cornell. The Spiderbot is a mobile motion-controlled robot. It is run on Arduino microcontrollers, stepper motors, ultrasonic sensors, and 3D-printed and lasercut parts.
Its primary function is to serve as a toy. Our goal was to create a toy with a contactless interface which translates bodily motion in space to visual output. There already exist many variations on remote-control toys and even contactless digital video games (for example, the Xbox Kinect) but there seems to be a lack of contactless physical toys with a solely creative (as opposed to goal-based) intent--this is what the Spiderbot aspires to be.

The Spiderbot is run on two Arduino microcontrollers. The user interacts with the control box by hovering their hands above the sensors on its lid, and the body responds by moving based on the input of the sensors. The wheels of the bot are powered by two stepper motors, and the two modules communicate with each other through TCP/IP.

Jack in the Box

The Jack-in-the-Box is a rapid prototype also created for the INFO 4320 course. It is a game--the LED on the box blinks two colors. The brighter color is the target color, and the user must match the two colors using the joystick control on the front face of the box. Once the user matches the colors, the head of the Jack pops out of the box. The Jack is moved by two servo motors.
